About

Aleena Mansur Shaikh is born in October 2010. She started riding from 2018, and as her father distinctly remembers it was almost end of March when this phenomena begin. No one in the family has ever had participated in Motocross or Supercross events. It all began with Mr. Mansur Shaikh taking his little princess on rides after he got his superbike. She loved the thrill of throwing all the care away. She started with 50CC Chinese bike through which he learnt the balance of biking and as this talent comes so naturally to Aleena, in just a month's time she was able to shift to Kawasaki KLX - 110CC bike. It has been less than a year as of today that Aleena has been riding and she aspires to participate in Dakar Rally.

MR. Mansur Shaikh has very clear view about his daughter and her love for motorsport - "Whatever she is determined to do, she has me on her side always. We are lucky to have support from family and friends. One more step to achieve yet in her path of success is sponsor's support that can strengthen her preparations and never let resources block her road. In all the high jumps she wants to take this one is critical as this can decide the future of a budding motorsport start that my daughter has set out to be."

Events & Achievements

So far Aleena has already added a few experiences of participating in various MOTOCROSS events

  • Participated and won third prize in FMSCI MOTOCROSS EVENT in Chickmagalur - Karnataka in ladies class on 24th June, 2018
  • Participated and won second prize in LIQUI MOLY Race in Kunigal in kids class on 27th January, 2019
  • Participated and came sixth in Pune SUPER CROSS event on 10th February, 2019
  • Came 2nd in Tumkur motocross event conducted by Bull racing team.

During this year Aleena wants to participate in MRF and GODSPEED Racing. Her coach and technical engineer Mohsin Pasha and Mujeeb Pasha are working constantly with Aleena to make her ready for the challenges she is aspiring to take on.

Inspiration

At Pune Supercross when she was asked what inspired her, with an angel like smile on her face Aleena pointed at her father and said - "I never realized when I asked Papa to ride faster, what actually I wanted was to talk to wind while on bike. Papa helped me transform my wishes into an aspiration that I can achieve."

Photos

In conversation with Aleena it stands out that she loves riding and wants to take high jumps and practice more so that she can grow up to be a PRO Rider by the age of 14 or 16. She says - Jumps, whoops, turns, dirt, motorcycles, they all conform into one glorious motorsport and makes me feel alive. I love watching my cares melt away when I hop on to a bike.
